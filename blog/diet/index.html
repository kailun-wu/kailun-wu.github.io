Diet

Sep 1st, 2014

I dropped 8 kg to 64 kg this summer by Atkins diet and occasional running. The basic idea is quit eating starch to force myself into consuming body fat. I saw this worked on my college roommate two years ago and decided to take some effort to control my own body this time. The key is no starch diet, a body scale and 2-mile running.

Is it painful? Yes, I was literally starving like never before! It’s only after one week did I become used to that state and cannot feel too hungry. But at some point I’m feeling like a vegetarian, clean and not demanding. Eating less also made me appreciate the food more and save time on cooking.

You can find recipes and debates on Atkins diet online so I’m not going to replicate anything here. What’s worth saying are the nicest foods I discovered that keep you full while having low calories.

  • Papaya: what a gift! Cut it into two half, clean the seeds and eat it with a spoon. Unbelievably easy and satisfying.

  • Strawberries: one box is enough for dinner, too acid sometimes, easy to get bored.

  • Steamed pumpkin: has relatively high GI value but satisfying nonetheless.

  • Miso soup: fast and delicious enough with low calories. Peeled tomatoes, miso paste, sesame oil, diced tofu. Optional: shredded Napa cabbage, diced low-calorie ham, seaweed, shrimp. Topped with minced scallion after boiling.

  • Shredded pork with green pepper: one of my best practiced recipes. Similar stir-fries: Cauliflower with shrimp, Eggplant with minced meat.

  • Salad with low-calorie dressing: without corns and bread of course. Cold, acid and harsh, you don’t want it too often.
